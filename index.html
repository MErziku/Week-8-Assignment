About Me

I am a dedicated biomedical engineering professional with both BSc and MSc degrees in the field. Currently serving as a lecturer at the University of Gondar, I'm involved in teaching, research, and community service.

My interest in biomedical engineering stems from a passion for leveraging technology to improve healthcare outcomes. Throughout my academic and professional journey, I have been dedicated to advancing medical technology and healthcare systems.

I have completed various significant projects, including developing a "Delivery Monitoring Machine" during my BSc and working on a "Simulation Framework for Magnetic Resonance Fingerprinting (MRF) Using Spiral Undersampling" for my MSc.
